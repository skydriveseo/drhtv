A Quality Viewing Experience

Delivering a quality viewing experience to a large audience spread across different geographical locations is one of the biggest challenges faced by sports networks. Different regions have different levels of network capacity available, which means that network congestion can be a major problem during major events. In order to combat this issue, broadcasters can typically use simulcasting, where a single stream is distributed across multiple networks.

Lack of Stadium Feel

Another challenge faced by sports broadcasters is something called "the stadium feel, i.e. the feeling of actually being there in the stadium when watching a game on TV. This issue can be solved by using multi-cam streaming, where content from multiple cameras can be combined into a single one.

Low Latency Streaming

This refers to the delay between the time an event takes place and the time you see it on your screen. It’s crucial to keep it as low as possible in order not to miss any action. The best latency is achieved by having your encoder close to the game itself - meaning in the stadium or in a truck parked right next to it. In most cases, however, broadcast trucks are located far away from the stadiums which means you have more than 1 second of latency - this is about the amount of time it takes for light to travel from the stadium to an encoder on the truck.
